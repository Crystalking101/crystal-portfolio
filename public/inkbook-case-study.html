The Problem

Mandarin's tone system has no real teacher online

Over 1.5 billion people are learning a language globally, and Mandarin Chinese is the most spoken native language in the world. Yet it remains one of the hardest for English speakers to learn primarily because of its 4-tone and 1 neutral system, where the same syllable means entirely different things depending on pitch contour. Most existing apps were built for European languages and awkwardly adapted for Mandarin. None of them provide real-time, personalized feedback on why a tone is wrong and how to fix it.

Market Opportunity

A $11B+ Mandarin learning market with an overlooked beginner problem

The global Mandarin learning market exceeds $10 billion and continues to grow as learners turn to mobile and AI-powered education.

While apps like Duolingo and HelloChinese focus on vocabulary, grammar, and gamification, beginners consistently struggle with one critical skill, hearing Mandarin tones correctly. A word spoken with the wrong tone can have a completely different meaning, yet few products are dedicated to tone ear training and listening confidence.

The demand is growing. The pain point is universal. But no category leader owns the tone training space.

InkBook helps learners hear Mandarin before they try to speak it, building confidence through focused tone recognition and listening practice.

Users

Built for a learner most apps overlook

InkBook is built for a specific kind of learner that most Mandarin apps overlook.

Primary
The Drama Learner
Adult beginner ages 18 to 35 who came to Mandarin through Chinese or Korean drama content. Motivated, emotionally invested, and wants a practical way to practice tones without feeling overwhelmed.
Secondary
The Frustrated Beginner
Any English speaking adult beginner who has tried Duolingo or Rosetta Stone and wants focused tone practice that those apps do not provide.
The Solution

A personal Mandarin tutor, available 24/7

InkBook is a Mandarin tone ear training web app powered by an AI Tone Coach Agent. It uses Azure Speech Services for real-time pronunciation scoring and listens to how a user pronounces a Mandarin tone. InkBook tells the user exactly what went wrong, and coaches them on how to fix it. Think of InkBook as a personal Mandarin tutor available 24/7.

Every other app tells you that you're wrong. InkBook tells you why, and shows you how to sound like you belong in the drama.

Design

A personal study journal, not a gamified app

InkBook was designed to feel like opening a personal study journal, not logging into another gamified app. The aesthetic is notebook and journal, lined paper texture, handwritten feel headers. This is a deliberate departure from Duolingo's loud, cartoonish energy. Learning a language tied to deep personal meaning, like a love of Chinese dramas, deserves a calmer and more intentional visual world.

The color palette reflects that intention. A warm cream background reads like aged notebook paper. A deep red accent carries real cultural significance in Chinese tradition. Deep ink black text and a forest green success state complete a palette that feels considered rather than decorative.

Typography pairs Playfair Display for headers, elegant and literary, with Plus Jakarta Sans for body text, clean and easy to read. Every tone is paired with an SVG pitch contour diagram showing the actual shape of the sound, flat, rising, dipping, or falling, so the visual learning reinforces the audio learning.

Features

Scoped with a strict MoSCoW framework

InkBook was scoped using a strict MoSCoW framework for a 6 day build window. Every decision below was deliberate.

Must Have — Shipped for submission
Chinese Name Generator
The user types their English name and receives a Mandarin equivalent with characters, Pinyin, and audio playback. This is the emotional entry point of the entire app.
4 Tone Introduction
A visual and audio introduction to all 4 Mandarin tones plus the neutral tone, with Pinyin notation and tone curve diagrams.
Tone Recording and Azure Speech Scoring
The user records a tone attempt, Azure Speech scores it, and the AI Tone Coach Agent explains what went wrong and how to fix it.
Cultural Drama Scene Context
Each tone is anchored to a short drama scene reference or cultural moment to make the learning memorable and personal.
Should Have — Built if time allowed
Encouragement Voice
The AI responds to a practice attempt in both English and Mandarin to keep the learner immersed.
Weak Spot Tracking
The AI tracks which tones the user struggles with most in session and serves more practice on those specific tones.
Won't Have — Explicitly out of scope
  • No mobile app. Web responsive only for this submission.
  • No full Mandarin curriculum. Tone training only, by design.
  • No social or community features.
  • No paid subscription or monetization.
Technical

Performance and privacy as hard requirements

InkBook was built on Next.js 14 with Tailwind CSS for a fully mobile responsive experience. GitHub Copilot was used throughout development to assist code generation, a requirement for the Creative Apps track.

Azure Speech Services handles both pronunciation scoring and text to speech playback, the core technical engine behind the entire learning experience. The Web Speech API provides browser native microphone access with no installation required, the user simply opens the app and starts speaking.

OpenRouter, using an LLM API, powers the AI coaching layer that turns a raw pronunciation score into a specific, personalized explanation of what went wrong and how to fix it. Supabase stores tone vocabulary, cultural context cards, and drama scene references. No user voice data is ever written to persistent storage. Audio is processed in memory and discarded immediately after scoring.

Performance and privacy were both treated as hard requirements rather than nice to haves. Azure Speech scoring was required to complete within 2 seconds of recording submission to maintain a natural practice flow, and the app was built to meet WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards for color contrast and keyboard navigation.

Outcome

Shipped, submitted, and fully functional

InkBook was built and submitted to the Microsoft Agents League Hackathon in a 6 day build window, under both the Creative Apps and Hack for Good tracks. As a solo founder and product manager, Crystal owned the full process, product strategy, design, and AI integration.

The app is fully functional. The name generator runs, the tone introduction plays native audio with visual pitch curves, and the Azure Speech scoring pipeline returns a real score from real microphone input, paired with AI coaching feedback.
