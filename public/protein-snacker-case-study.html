Overview

The snack discovery app for macro trackers

Protein Snacker is a mobile app for iOS and Android that helps health-conscious users discover high-protein, low-carb and low-sugar snacks, protein drinks and powders tailored to their personal dietary goals.

Powered by a real-time filter engine and a 4-layer nutrition database, Protein Snacker eliminates the frustration of label-scanning in grocery aisles and endless scrolling on Amazon. A friendly panda mascot guides users through every step of the snack discovery journey.

The app is currently in active development with a target launch of Q4 2026.

The Problem

Finding compliant snacks is surprisingly hard

People who prioritize protein intake have no fast, goal-aware way to discover snacks that match their exact macro requirements.

Existing apps like MyFitnessPal focus on logging what you already eat, not helping you find what you should be eating. Grocery apps surface products by brand, not nutrition goals. Amazon returns thousands of results with no way to filter by macros.

The result is frustrating. Users either spend excessive time reading labels in grocery aisles, default to the same 3 to 4 familiar products, or miss a huge category of better-fit options they never even knew existed.

"I know my macros. I just need someone to tell me what I can actually eat."

Market Opportunity

A $5.3 billion market with no dedicated discovery tool

The global protein snack market reached $5.3 billion in 2024 and is growing at 9.1% annually through 2034, with North America leading at 38.6% of global revenue.

70% of consumers are actively trying to increase their protein intake, and protein bars alone account for 37% of the total product category. Online retail is one of the fastest growing channels, making a mobile-first discovery experience especially well timed.

The demand is there. The behavior is shifting. Yet no dedicated tool exists to help consumers find the right high-protein snack for their specific goals in a fast and frictionless way. Protein Snacker is built for exactly that gap.

Competitive Landscape

Where Protein Snacker sits in the market

Every alternative requires users to either log food, scroll through unfiltered results or rely on trends. Protein Snacker is the only tool that helps you find the right snack for your exact goals in seconds.

The competition helps you track what you already eat. Protein Snacker helps you discover what you should be eating.

Target Users

Who we are building for

Three distinct users with different relationships to protein and fitness — each requiring a different product response.

Primary Persona
The Macro Tracker

Age 22 to 38. Gym-goer, athlete or fitness enthusiast who tracks macros in MyFitnessPal or a similar app. Frustrated by the time it takes to find compliant snacks. Values convenience and variety. Spends $30 to $80 per month on protein snacks and supplements.

Core need: Find a snack that hits my numbers in under 60 seconds without opening a nutrition label.
Secondary Persona
The Health-Conscious Professional

Age 28 to 45. Busy professional eating clean but not strictly tracking. Interested in reducing sugar and carbs. Shops at Whole Foods, Target and Amazon. Wants quick product validation without deep nutrition coaching.

Tertiary Persona
The Supplement Seeker

Age 18 to 30. College student or early career professional actively researching protein powders and ready-to-drink protein drinks. Price-conscious but willing to pay for the right product. Heavy social media user likely to share finds.

Design Process

From concept to prototype

Protein Snacker is currently in active development. Early design decisions were driven by one core principle: make snack discovery feel fast, friendly and effortless.

The design process began on the iPad, sketching out the core screens before writing a single line of code. Each frame focused on the key user decisions including setting macro targets, browsing results, scanning barcodes and managing goals, ensuring the product logic was solid before moving into development.

The prototyping phase involved iterating through several visual directions, from dark mode to light, before arriving at the playful pastel theme that best matched the friendly and approachable tone of Protein Snacker. A panda mascot was chosen as the face of the brand and will be used consistently throughout the app, with placeholder logos in place while the final version is finalized.

The design system uses a cream to mint gradient background, Fredoka for headings and Plus Jakarta Sans for body text to create a warm and approachable feel without sacrificing utility. A panda mascot guides users through onboarding and celebrates key moments throughout the app experience.

Data Architecture

A 4-layer data strategy built for scale

The database strategy is one of the most technically differentiated decisions in this product.

Protein Snacker uses a layered multi-source data architecture designed to maximize snack coverage and barcode match rate while keeping API costs near zero at launch.

1
Manual curated database — Supabase

500 hand-verified hero snacks stored directly in Supabase. Fast, reliable and always available with zero API dependency. This is what users see first. Every product has a full nutrition panel, product image, category tags, dietary flags and purchase links.

2
Open Food Facts — synced nightly

A free, open-source database of 2.5M+ products synced nightly into Supabase. Freely cacheable with no restrictions. Covers broad packaged snack coverage with barcode mapping. Products are validated on ingest — all core macro fields required before surfacing.

3
USDA FoodData Central — synced monthly

Government-maintained nutrition data synced monthly. Used as a data quality reference and tiebreaker for generic foods when sources disagree. Covers 250,000 foods. No API key required.

4
FatSecret API — live calls only

The primary live API called only when Supabase layers return no result. Selected for its 5,000 free daily calls (5x more than competitors), 90%+ barcode match rate and 2.3M+ verified food items. Trusted by Amazon, Fitbit and Samsung. Most users will never trigger a paid API call.

Risks

Known risks and mitigations

Data quality from crowd-sourced sources

Open Food Facts data varies in accuracy since it is crowd-sourced. Products with missing or conflicting macro data could surface bad results.

Mitigation: Validation rules on ingest require all core macro fields before a product is surfaced. Failed records are quarantined, not deleted.
API costs at scale

FatSecret's free tier covers 5,000 daily calls. Heavy usage could push into paid territory unexpectedly.

Mitigation: The 3-layer Supabase cache means most users never trigger a live API call. Upgrade to FatSecret Premier when daily calls consistently exceed 4,000.
App Store approval delays

Apple and Google review processes can delay launch timelines unpredictably.

Mitigation: Using Expo to streamline the submission process and submitting early with buffer time before the Q3 target.
Cold start on catalog

Users may not find their specific niche snack on day one, creating a poor first experience.

Mitigation: The 500-snack curated database ensures a strong experience for the most popular products before the broader database fills in via API layers.
Freemium conversion

Users may stay on the free tier indefinitely if the upgrade prompt does not land at the right moment.

Mitigation: Free tier is intentionally limited on saves and lists so power users feel the upgrade prompt naturally through usage, not through hard paywalls.
